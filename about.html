My Story

The Player Behind
the Pieces

I'm Miguel Mosweu, an 18-year-old from Botswana with a story that starts with a stumble and keeps moving forward. Like most students, life hasn't been a smooth opening — but every setback taught me something I couldn't have learned any other way.

"The pain of a C+ became the engine behind a 45-point BGCSE."

Moving between schools in my junior years disrupted my routine and my mindset. I was chasing freedom instead of focus, and my JCE results showed it — a grade C+ that I still carry as a reminder. Seeing my mother's disappointment hit harder than any failed move on the chessboard. It rewired me.

In Senior school, I locked in completely. The result? 45 points in my BGCSE — enough to get me into exactly the course I wanted: Business Intelligence and Data Analytics at Botswana Accountancy College. The world is running on data and AI — I want to be right in the middle of that revolution.

Outside of coursework, I'm a passionate chess player and football enthusiast. Chess especially has shaped how I think — it demands concentration, patience, and the ability to plan several moves ahead. It stresses me out, it humbles me, and it teaches me more about strategy than any textbook. My goal is to reach the International Master or Grand Master level — and every free moment I get goes toward that.
